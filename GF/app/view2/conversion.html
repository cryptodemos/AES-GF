From ` Number ` To ` Polynomial `

A polynomial with coefficients drawn from a set ` S ` can be represented by assigning a numeric value. For instance, the polynomial ` x^3+x^2+1 ` with coefficients from ` GF(2) ` can be represented as ` 1101_2 ` in binary representation, or ` 13_10 ` in decimal representation, or ` d_{HEX} ` in hexadecimal representation. Below, we demonstrate how a numeric representation (for a given choice of radix – e.g. decimal, binary, hex, octal) can be interpreted as a polynomial with coefficients drawn from ` Z_n (n=2, … 16) ` .
